Environmental Science & Carbon Dynamics

The Greenhouse Effect

Greenhouse gases (CO₂, CH₄, N₂O) envelope the atmospheric crust, acting as a structural solar trap. As individual energy generation and internal combustion processes intensify, industrial accumulation accelerates thermal atmospheric changes, leading to long-term climate variance.

Carbon Equivalencies (CO₂e)

Different greenhouse metrics have distinct Global Warming Potential values. To streamline international environmental telemetry data, global systems convert all variable gases into a single benchmark coefficient known as Carbon Dioxide Equivalent (CO₂e).
